Electromagnetism is the invisible force powering our modern world, from the hum of a transformer to the spin of a hard drive. But how do you *see* the invisible? How do you test, optimize, and validate a magnetic field before you ever build a physical prototype?

The answer lies in simulation, and one of the most powerful and accessible tools for this is **FEMM (Finite Element Method Magnetics)**. FEMM allows you to visualize magnetic fields, calculate forces, and analyze performance right on your computer.

---

## Who Is This Session For?

This isn't just academic theory. FEMM is a practical tool that solves real-world problems across multiple fields:

* **Electrical & Power Engineers:** Analyze transformer core saturation, calculate winding losses, quantify torque in motors, and predict force-stroke curves for solenoids.
* **Electronics Engineers:** Model custom inductors for SMPS, test Hall-effect sensors, and analyze stray EMI fields.
* **Mechatronics & Robotics:** Model the electromagnetic core of linear actuators and bridge the gap between electrical drive circuits and mechanical output.
* **Students & Researchers:** Visualize Maxwell's Equations in action and prototype designs on a budget.

---

## What You Will Learn

In this session, we move from concept to application:

* **Core Concepts:** An intuitive explanation of how the Finite Element Method works for magnetics.
* **Interface Mastery:** A guided tour of the FEMM workspace.
* **Live Modeling:** We set up, mesh, solve, and analyze a practical electromagnetic problem from start to finish.
* **Result Interpretation:** Learn to read flux plots and force calculations to make informed design decisions.
